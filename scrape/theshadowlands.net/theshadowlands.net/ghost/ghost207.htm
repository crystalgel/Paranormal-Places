And it was'nt no cat.  I would only catch a glimpse, and it would scurry away, I never saw what it really looked like.   My sister said she saw it once, she was washing her hair in the bathroom sink (the bathroom door was right below the opening), when she finished she wrapped a towel arond her head, turned to walk out of the room, and standing in the hall was what she described as a small creature, about 2 feet tall, pionted ears and all black.  She said it acted suprized she saw it, and moved sideways real fast and dissapeared.  My mom says that sometimes she would goo into her bedroom and see an indention in the blankets and pillow of a where a small body had lain.

lively game of some sort, laughing and having a lot of fun. . . with no-one
visible around.

"malfunctioned" like that anywhere but THAT house.

replace the fireplace doors with something other than glass?

earthly sojourn, and go to the place she was meant to go. . .  back to the
God who created her, and the heavenly home he has made for her.]

Haunted In Santee, CA

By: afox@arrowheadgrp.com

I have always thought that ghosts exist.  I never had any proof to speak of
(until recently), it was just something I believed.  I moved to Santee,
California about 8 months ago and have since obtained what I believe to be
proof of ghosts.
I share a small, one bedroom apartment with my husband in the small town of
Santee.  Ever since first arriving in the apartment I have had the feeling
of never really being alone.  My husband is in the NAVY and is often gone
for 3 or 4 days at a time and I am left "alone" in the apartment.  I always
feel a presence though---something watching me.
The first thing that happened that got the attention of both  (my husband
and myself) of us was the day the light in the kitchen turned off by itself.
We were both standing in the kitchen, preparing dinner, when the light over
head just went out.  We at first thought the bulb had burned out, but upon
investigation found that the switch had been flipped.  We were the only ones
in the house and were facing each other--away from the switch--when this
occurred.  Can you say, goose bumps?!
The next thing that happened, which convinced both of us that we were not
alone in the house, is the morning we awoke late for work to find the entire
apartment in disarray.  First it was odd that we woke up late.  We always
set the alarm clock for 4:30 and are both paranoid about being late so we
both check it before going to bed to make sure it's set right.  When we woke
up this particular morning, the alarm clock had been turned off and was set
for 6 in the morning.  When we left our bedroom (the door was closed,
something neither of us ever does) to start hastily getting ready for work,
we were alarmed by what we found.
I had been doing laundry when we went to bed.  When we awoke, the dryer door
was open, with clothes strewn through out the hallway and the washer lid was
open, revealing water and laundry sitting in it---it was opened before the
spin cycle obviously.  The TV in the living room was on---muted however--
along with the radio, VCR and cable box. All the clocks had different times
on them---none of them correct--- and every light in the place was on.   All
windows and doors were closed and locked, so no living person was in the
apartment.  The other thing is, how did we sleep through all this??
The most recent thing has been this last week and only affects me.  Every
night, for the last 7 nights, I have awoken at exactly 3:32AM, startled and
frightened.  Every night I get the sensation that I'm being watched, but I
can't find anyone in the apartment and turning on all the lights does not
make the feeling diminish.  The "episode" generally lasts until 4:45 or so
when the feeling of alarm just goes away and I can go back to sleep.  The
first five days this occurred I was home alone and figured I was just not
sleeping well with out my husband.  The last two nights he has been home and
sound asleep through it all.  Today he leaves again and I'm curious to see
if it continues.
I don't know for sure if it's the spirit in my apartment that's causing my
sleeping problem or something else??  Other than this last week, there has
never been a steady stream of things going on.  The light switch thing was
back in December of 2000 and the crazy morning was in February.  Any insight
from anyone would be greatly appreciated.  I'm sick of being awake every
night--I'm getting tired!
