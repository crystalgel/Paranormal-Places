Dancing in the Dark
 

By: PaleShadesOfGrey@aol.com
 
I really enjoy reading the stories of others who have had ghostly encounters
and thought I would share one of my first, memorable encounters with a spirit
- outside my family (we've had plenty of family members paying us ghostly
visits, including pets).  This encountered happened when I was about 12-13
years old.  My mother, my brothers and sister, my friend Bridget and I had
gone to visit my cousin and his family in a small rural town in Connecticut.
Their home was slightly back from the road in a well wooded area.  It was a
new house, no prior owners.  Their neighbors were a fair distance off on
either side with lots of trees separating them (zoning is 3 acres in this
area).  Later that evening when we were getting ready to head back home my
friend Bridget and I 'called' the front seat for the ride - back in the 70's
kids could actually ride in the front seat of the car untethered...!   To
ensure we got the front seat before anyone else laid claim to it, Bridget and
I went outside and were leaning against the car, talking.  It was around 9pm,
dark, the only light coming from the porch light and the windows of the
house.  Out there sharing the dark with the crickets and the bullfrogs, a
sudden movement in my peripheral vision caused me to turn to the right where
I saw something totally unbelievable.  It was a puff of mist, probably the
size of a cocker spaniel.  It's not unusal to see little pockets of mist
lingering in spots in the woods at night I'm sure, but THIS little puff of
mist was moving through the trees at a fair pace.  Well, moving doesn't
exactly explain it. It was weaving in and out and around the tree trunks,
swooshing up and around and down again, almost as if it were playing. It was
definitely a happy, playful movement. There was no wind that night - and even
if there were it would dissipate the mist, not cause it to move like that
while the less talented mist lay still at the base of the trees. Wide-eyed I
turned toward an equally wide-eyed Bridget, who was looking at me. I said "Do
you see that?!" and she said "Yes. You see it, too?!"  With that we both went
running up the steps back in to the house.  By the time we managed to
convince the others to come outside for a look, the dancing mist was gone -
all that remained were its less animate friends lying low to the ground.
Needless to say, no one believed us at the time and we were teased about it
for quite a while.  All I can say is thank God Bridget was with me to witness
the performance or I might have been inclined to agree with the skeptics that
I had imagined it.  That was one of my first encounters that I vividly
recall. There have been many since that time - some definitely not so funny.
I believe there are some people who, for whatever reason, are more sensitive
to these experiences, and that spirits can feel this and lean toward contact
with them for that reason.  It can run in families, too.  My brothers, my
sister, my father and I were all sensitive this way. My mother and older
brother were not.  One strange experience:  Growing up I had many encounters
with 'ghosts'.  Being ridiculed by my mother whenever I brought them up made
me keep the smaller ones to myself (I only yelled about the ones that really
scared me!).  One incident in particular was the man in the bedroom.  There
is one bedroom in our home that for a time I used as my own.  Some nights I'd
be trying to fall asleep - or even be asleep and be awakened by the sensation
that there was someone standing very close to the bed staring at me.  I
didn't have this feeling in any other bedroom in the house. I never mentioned
it to anyone.  One night I even heard a voice say "Good night." to me. My dog
growled and my mother called out from her bedroom asking me what I had said!
Eventually I married and moved away.  My sister and her husband bought a new
house and while waiting for everything to clear came to stay with my mother
for a short period of time.  They stayed in that room.  One day my sister
phoned me to tell me something strange.  She said she was uncomfortable
sleeping in that room - that she swears she feels someone is in the room with
them at night, standing by the bed in the dark staring at them.  She said she
didn't mention it to her husband, afraid he would ridicule the whole thing.
Then one morning her husband told her "You know, I'm embarrassed to say this,
but that bedroom gives me the creeps. I swear at night I wake up sometimes
feeling someone is standing right next to the bed staring down at me." To her
surprise I told her I never mentioned it before but I got the same feeling in
there when I lived in that room. Three people, all getting the same feeling
in the room, afraid to say anything to anyone for fear of being laughed at!
I'm back with my mother now after separating from my husband.  I sleep in
that same room, but every night before I turn off the lights I think "I don't
want to see you, I don't want to feel you, I don't want to hear you, or even
THINK you. In the name of God go away and leave me alone."  It seems to work
- I haven't had any strange experiences in there since I started that nightly
mantra.
