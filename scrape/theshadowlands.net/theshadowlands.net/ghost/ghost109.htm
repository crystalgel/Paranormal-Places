The Story of Simon
 
 

By: Caristione@aol.com

    My high-school sweetheart, Tony, had an older sister, Angelina, who
related this story to me.  I think it needs to be shared here.

    Shortly after Tony's birth, when Angelina was 6, the family bought and
moved into a large old farmhouse off the beaten track in a very historical
area.  About six months after taking residence, Angie began to notice strange
things.  Being a six-year-old tomboy, Angie was a fearless child, and never
thought for a moment that she should be frightened by these happenings.  The
television would go on or off when Angie was alone in the room.  The family
heirloom bentwood rocker in her bedroom would rock whenever Angie laid down
for the night.  Her mother tells me that Angie would sit and talk when she
was alone in a room.  Angie tells me that many times she would know someone
was in the room with her.  She couldn't see anything, or feel anything, but
she just knew.  The "entity" also seemed very protective of the baby,
covering him up in his crib when he kicked off the blanket, or turning off
the light in the nursery when Angie or her parents accidentally (or
purposely!) left it on.

    After a year, Angie began to call this entity Simon, partly after a
character in her favourite book, and partly because, as she says, "The name
just came to me one day".  She told her parents about Simon.  Her mother
partly believed her.  Her father chalked it up to an imaginary friend.  Simon
was always with her, she told me, no matter where she went, but his presence
was always strongest in the house and in the yard.  He never spoke to her,
nor did he ever manifest himself to her.

    As Angelina grew up, she started to question Simon's existence, and
almost talked herself into believing that her father was right; Simon was
only a young girl's imaginary friend.  But when the day came, when Angie was
16, to move out of the old farmhouse, Simon seemed a little confused.  She
stood in her empty bedroom, the rest of the family outside loading the truck.
 A sudden breeze blew into the room, when the window was firmly shut.  It
seemed to circle the room, then go out the door to the next bedroom, which
had been Tony's room.  It came back in, a bit stronger this time, and circled
the room again.  It blew straight at Angie, and in her own words, "Just
dissipated... never actually "left"... as if I had absorbed it..."

    As the years passed, Angie became a beautiful, headstrong, willful
full-blooded Italian woman with scores of suitors.  She says that whenever
she dated a man who turned out to be a bad seed, she would feel sudden
attacks of anxiety, or what she called "bad vibes".  She would always end the
relationship soon afterwards.  It was only after she became engaged that she
came to believe that it was Simon, protecting her from the "wrong men".
Other than that, Simon stayed quiet.

    Angolan is married now, a mother of three.  I spoke to her a year ago,
before she and her family moved to England.  She reported that she had
finally worked up the nerve to go back home and research the farmhouse they
had lived in.  What she found made cold fingers crawl up her back.  The house
had been built in 1842, by a young farmer for his new bride.  The young man
enlisted to fight for the Union shortly after, and a short year later, left
his bride a widow.  The young farmer's name was Simon.

    She also reported that Simon had all but disappeared from her life.  His
last visit was on the night she went into labor with her firstborn.  It was 3
o'clock in the morning when the pain started, so intense all she could do was
cry and wail.  Her husband was working the late shift, and wouldn't be home
for another couple of hours.  Unable to move from the bed to the phone, she
just laid there and screamed.  Finally, in a moment of desperation, she
mumbled, "Simon, help me!"  Twenty minutes later, the pain lessened enough to
allow her to walk to the phone and call her husband.

    Her first child was christened James Alexander Simon.
