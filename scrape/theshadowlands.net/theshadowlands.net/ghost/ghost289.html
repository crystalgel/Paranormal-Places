My Daughter and I

By: acu1970@charter.net

In 1995, I moved to a small town in Nebraska with my boyfriend. The house was his mothers and fathers, and they had used it as a rental property. Being elderly, they did not check on the condition of the house while they had renters, as they lived about an hour from the property. We drove thru town for them and found the property abandoned and virtually in a shambles. Water damage...carpet destroyed...garbage through the entire house. It was terrible. We agreed to move there and clean up the house and resore it to living condition. For the first month or so....we spent around the clock cleaning and getting rid of trash and debris. My boyfriend was around when he wasnt working. Nothing unusual happened.
He then got a job trucking over the road and would be gone a few weeks at a time. It was hard being alone, so I thought that the things I was experiencing were just things I was making up in my head. Just my mind playing tricks on me. I now believe that wasnt the case.
At first, I just felt like I wasnt alone in the house. That feeling you get when the hair on your neck stands up and you get the chills down your spin. When I would be sitting in the main room, it felt as if someone was walking up behind me. I would turn around to find nothing was there. But after a few minutes I would get the same feeling and constantly be looking over my shoulder.  I would just try to laugh it off....but the feeling never left me. The first time I really felt my fears were valid was very late one night as I got up to use the bathroom. As I walked out of the bedroom and thru the kitchen, I could hear footsteps coming up behind me. As if someone was stomping. When I got to the bathroom I slammed the door and started to cry. I was so scared.  I stood there for what felt like a lifetime. When I didnt hear anything for a while, I opened the door and ran back to the bedroom, jumped into bed and closed my eyes tight. All night I heard a scratching type noise coming from only one wall in the bedroom. It was the wall where the basement steps were.  It sounds crazy, but from that night on, I locked the door that led to the basement. Because I felt a terrible uneasiness walking past it. I would share my story with Eric when he would come home. But he just laughed at me and said that it was my imagination. Little did he know....
A few weeks later, he was home for the weekend and we were getting ready to go out. I was in the bathroom getting ready and he was in the bedroom.  He walked thru the kitchen and all of a sudden the footsteps came up behind him. The same loud stomping sounds I had experienced. Eric froze and I swear the color drained from his face. He looked at me, his eyes wide. I asked him if he believed me now and he said he did. He had the same uneasy feeling when he walked past the basement door. One he didnt feel prior to the stomping. He didnt laugh at my stories anymore.
When I would walk thru the house, I would feel as if something was following me. This feeling would be day or night. Except at night, the house would turn ice cold. And at night was when most of the things happened. One night as I was walking thru the dining room, I turned around and thought I saw the figure of a man.  He was following the path I was taking. I closed my eyes and asked him to please stop following me. When I opened my eyes, I still had the feeling something was there, but I couldnt see anything. Was it my imagination?
We lived in the house from September of 1995 till March of 1996. I found out I was pregnant in February of 1996 and we began looking for a house to buy. I told Eric I didnt want to live in that house because it made me feel very stressed, and I was already a high risk pregnancy. We moved in March and I remember saying to Eric that I was glad that our "spirit" couldnt go with us. I also wish I could say this is where my story ends....but I cant.
Everything was pretty quiet after the move.  I didnt have the uneasy feeling in the new house and I felt very comfortable. The baby room was at the back of the house. The only downside to that room was that the door to the crawlspace was in there and we would have to lift the carpet to get to it. The water heater and fuse box were down there. So I had no real reason to be down there.  Emma was born in October of that year. She was a wonderful baby!  She would sleep thru the night and was never much trouble. She started talking at about 8 months old. At about 9 months is when she would start waking in the night screaming. I would run in and she would be standing in her crib, just sobbing.  This would happen at about the same time every night. So I figured it was just her schedule changing and it was her way of getting my attention. At about a year old is when she would start pointing at the floor in her room saying "man mama, man". And at about 18 months was the first time I realized something wasnt right. I was changing Emma diaper in my room and we were giggling and playing when Emma stopped cold and started to stare at the bedroom entry way. I asked Emma what she was looking at and she said "Man". I felt the hairs on my neck stand up. I asked Emma what the man was doing and she replied "Looking at you". I told Emma to tell the man to go away. She said "Go man!". After I was done with her diaper, she got down off the bed and walked over to the couch by the large window in the living room. She was staring out the window "at the man" she said.  "He's looking at me".
Emma would still wake from time to time at night, screaming out for me. When she was about 3 is when she told me the man would crawl out of her floor and stare at her.  I asked her how long he had been doing this. She said "for a long time". I believe that it had been happening since she was an infant, and that it was the man that would make her scream for me. I dont know if we brought him with us from the old house. I dont know if things like that are possible. But the fact that my daughter saw a man and that I feel that what I saw in the first house was a man, seems to be too much of a common thread.
She saw the man from time to time. He didnt seem to scare her unless it was in the night when he would come thru her floor. I had my second daughter in August of 1999. She didnt have the same experiences as Emma. In 2001, my marriage ended and I moved back to my home town. When I was leaving the house, I said out loud that the spirit was not welcome to come with us and laughed when Emma said the same thing. I knew I wasnt alone in my experiences. Nothing happened to us after that. Although my ex husband said that the pipes in the house burst shortly after I left and that he came home to find the house in shambles. He was still driving truck over the road when we seperated and the house would stand empty when he was gone.  I dont know if someone maybe broke in...or if our "spirit" just didnt like being alone. Eric sold the house about a year later.
We dont talk about the man at all.  But, it made me believe. And made me have respect for things unknown.
