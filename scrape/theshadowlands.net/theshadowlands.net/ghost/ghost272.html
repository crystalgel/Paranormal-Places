All houses in the middle of nowhere are a little spooky.  This one was too, at first.  After the initial exploration, without flashlights, it was determined that there were no holes in the floors to fall through, therefore it was safe to play hiden-go-seek.  We had played in the house a few times before my experience.  One night (fall, between 8 and 10), I chose to hide in a bedroom closet up stairs.  I heard voices in the bedroom, so I was extra quiet.  Normally, nobody spoke during the game anyway, but I thought someone was caught and talking.  I listened very intently.  I could hear indistinct voices of at least a dozen people chatting, men and women, clothing rustling, mirth in the voices.  It sounded as though there was a social party happening.  I could tell the voices were coming from the room, so I looked into the room.  Nobody, no movement, nothing.  The voices stopped as soon as I looked out of the closet.  I first thought that I had strained so hard to listen for the seeker that I started to have a delusion.  Then, goosebumps raised, I high-tailed out if the closet and found my father and related the story.  He said that everybody else (five people) heard the same thing while hiding in that closet.  We continued a few more games that night, but I avoided that closet.
I returned to the house 8 years later during the day with my best friend.  Cattle had left inches on manure on the first floor and the house was deteriorating fast.  The house felt as empty as it had been for the past decades.  When I inspected the nearby chicken coop, I found the skeletons of three chicken still in the roosts.  In a land where racoons, foxes, and coyotes are everywhere, no animal remains are left untouched.  These were.  Very odd.  The house was torn down sometime within 5 years after that.  End of experience 1.
Experience 2, age 24:  I live in Rolla, Missouri.  An abandoned house, now next to Lowe's, had piqued my interest to explore.  I walked to it one Sunday afternoon and explored the out buildings first, then the house itself.  The house is a one-storied, boarded over, wood frame house.  I was determined not to be stopped in my exploration by plywood covered doors.  I managed to peel the back door cover open like a door, crawl under the broken lower panel on the back door and enter the back of the house.  The small entryway was spray painted blue, royal blue.  I unlocked the door and opened it to let more light in.  The back door was blue too.  Strange.  I have explored several dozen abandoned houses in my life and none had blue entryways.  The entire house was pitch black even though it was bright outside.  The kitchen was to the right, so I let my eyes adjust and stepped into the kitchen.  That is when it came at me.  As I stared into the blackness, some of the light around the boarded windows trickled through.  It was still very dark, but something menacing was exerting itself towards me.  My vision rippled, like heat waves, so, hair standing on end I quickly stepped back to the blue entryway.  It didn't follow.  I was determined not to be chased away.  I strapped on my belt of faith (figuratively) and stepped into the kitchen again.  It was waiting for me.  In the name of Jesus Christ, I bound it from harming me and told it that Jesus had forgiven its sins and that God would have mercy on It.  It left.  The house suddenly became brighter.  I could see the trash strewn on the floor, the peeling wall paper, I could even read the pages from torn books.  I explored the entire house.  Not much to see, except the 6 foot by 6 foot hole torn in the bedroom floor.  It looked like a hiding area or something.  Strange.  I left.  I returned a month later.  The bad presence was definitely gone for good.  The house still stands behind the Autos of Yester Year museum.
