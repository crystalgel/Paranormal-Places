Dromoland Castle story

By: tj-sakoor@rogers.com
 
 

    My wife and I had just found your site and read a few of the stories.  I am not someone who readily believed in ghosts, until my own encounter with some "unexplainable experiences".  My wife and I were married on December 18, 1997 and spent our two week honeymoon in Ireland.  We stayed in Dublin, Waterford and Limerick.  Dromoland castle is about one hour away from Limerick and my sister decided to put us up for two nights at Dromoland castle as a wedding present.  Upon arriving, we were quite impressed to find out that she had put us in the Presidential Suite (where president's of the U.S. have stayed when visiting there).  The Presidential Suite is in a separate part of the castle, away from the rest of the guest rooms.  As you can imagine, there is a lot of empty space (hallways, conference rooms, etc.) around the suite and it was quite unusual to have that much room to ourselves.  I am not normally one to scare easily, but given the unfamiliar territory, I and my wife found it a bit unsettling.  Our first night was pretty uneventful...or so we thought.  After a wonderful dinner we decided to check out parts of the hotel.  The weather was horrible (the worst Ireland had seen in 50 years...high winds and rain had knocked out a lot of Ireland's power throughout the country for part of our honeymoon).  There was power in the castle, but given the surroundings and the sound of the wind and rain hitting the roof and windows, it made things a little scarier.  We had no idea what was about to happen.
    We came upon the games room, which had ping pong and a snooker table right beside each other.  The snooker table was occupied by two men so my wife and I decided to play ping pong.  About halfway through our game, even though we were moving at a semi-physical rate, we both realized that it became very cold in the room.  Then something strange happened.  One of my shots went off the table and under the snooker table.  I retrieved the ball and then realized that the men were no longer in the room.  I asked my wife if she had seen them leave and she immediately turned white!!!  The only exit/entrance in the room is beside the ping pong table, so they would have had to walk right past us to get out.  A cold chill went up my spine and my wife and I dropped our paddles and headed back to our suite.  We spent part of the night talking about what had happened and I had convinced my wife, as well as myself, that the events were nothing out of the ordinary.  The heat could have been lost in the room due to the weather, after all, we had been experiencing brief power outages for most of our honeymoon.  Secondly, we could have been so enthralled in our game (and let's face it...we were on our honeymoon and consumed with each other), that we may have not noticed the men leaving the room.  My wife agreed with me and we enjoyed a decent nights sleep.
    During breakfast the next morning, I casually mentioned the "heat loss" in the game room to our waiter.  He had told us that the hotel is equipped with emergency generators and a loss of power or heat is improbable.  My wife looked at me and I could see in her eyes that she did not want to go back into the game room again.  We spent the day roaming the grounds of Dromoland castle and my wife started to feel a bit more comfortable.  After dinner, we spent the majority of the evening in the bar of Dromoland castle, talking to the bartender, waitresses and other guests.  Even though my wife had not wanted to talk about it, I asked some of the staff about the history behind Dromoland.  They shared some information and also indicated that they had never experienced anything unusual.  The bartender had said that he had heard a few stories from other guests, but chalked it up to "their minds playing tricks on them".  We retired to our suite for the remainder of the night.  About 3am in the morning, my wife woke me up in a panic.  She had awoken to the sounds of footsteps outside our bedroom.  I sat up and listened for a couple of minutes, but heard nothing.  I calmed my wife down and just as I was trying to fall back asleep, I heard some noises to.  They noise was "tin-ish", sort of sounding like metal banging against stone.  I listened for a minute or so and decided to check out what was going on.  With my wife clinging to the back of my pajamas, we ventured out into the hall.  The noise was now coming from the conference room down the hall.  We walked towards the door, when all of a sudden the door slammed shut in front of us.  We ran straight back to our room, locked the door and stayed up watching T.V. for the rest of the night.  Even though there was nothing to be afraid of, we never felt threatened in anyway, I can honestly say that I have never been that freaked out in all my life.  We spent about two more hours listening to the strange noises before they stopped.
    When we checked out the next morning, we had asked the desk clerk about the conference room.  Something he said sent one last "Dromoland chill" up my spine.  He had told me that the conference room is always locked when it is not being used and there is nobody using it during the early morning hours, not even cleaning staff.  While I can logically explain our experience on the first night (even though I still wonder about it), there is no doubt that on our second night, we had an encounter with "spirits".  I was glad to leave, but if I were to go back to Ireland again, I think I would go back to Dromoland for one more night, just see if anything else unusual happens.

Ghost cat?

By: Bunnienoname@aol.com
 
My name is Stephanie Hall and I live in Wimborne, Dorset England.
In the summer of 2000, our oldest cat Polly, who was 16, died of massive liver failure. She had been in the family as long as I had (I was 15 at the time) and we were all very upset by her death.
About two weeks after she had died my mum came downstairs for breakfast, I was still in bed at the time. She had just sat down at the table when she heard a scratching noise coming from the cupboards in the kitchen, she looked but was unable to find any explanation. She sat down again and heard the sound once more, she was about to get up and look again when the catflap, situated right next to the dining room table, opened and shut quickly as if a cat had just run through it. Our back garden is very sheltered, and ever when there has been wind blowing right at the bac door, the catflap has never opened in the wind, plus this was a fine late summer day and there was only a very slight breeze.
Over the next few days and weeks, the catflap went in and out, and our other two cats would often jump off the sofa's in the lounge and walk to the door into the kitchen and stare at the back door, but we could never see any explanation for this.
My mother experienced most of these things and I believe this is because she was the one that Polly trusted most (she was a very timid cat).
However, I myself would sometimes experience things, like in the middle of the night or when I was trying to get to sleep I would feel what felt like a cat jumping lightly on to my bed and walked up the duvet towards my head. We have had no more experiences since we buried her ashes in the back garden. Perhaps this laid her spirit to rest, or perhaps she was just making sure we were going to cope without her.
