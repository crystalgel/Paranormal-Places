within the short time it would take to answer the phone and return to the

a couch cushion pulled itself onto the floor, and my dog began to howl.
Then it stopped--it had all happened in a matter of seconds.  needless to say, it
scared the hell out of me, because similar things had been happening to me

nightmares in which unknown older male and female voices asked me to come
with them to "the bottom."
my grandmother told me calmly that i was doubtlessly being followed by a
poltergeist, and that under no circumstances should i pay attention to the
occurrences.  in fact, she warned me that if objects moved around, i should
leave the room until they stopped, and then quietly come back later to pick
them up.  i did this, and eventually the craziness ended for awhile.

suppose my question is WHY?  why is he coming to ME?  i haven't asked him,

By: darren1@mindspring.com

I have been telling this story for about eight years now--since the night it happened.  I was a freshman at Bellarmine College, a small Catholic college only slightly larger than a high school at the time.  The fall semester had just begun and, since the weather was warm, my friends and I decided to play a few games of volleyball on the sand courts.  After two or three games, I announced that I needed to be getting home because it was nearly dark and the headlight on my motorcycle was broken.  In an effort to avoid a possible traffic violation, I decided to cut through the park, which surrounds the Collins Estate, a large mansion--now city park headquarters.
I was cruising slowly up the narrow carraigeway with the woods on my right and the rolling front property on my left.  The carraigeway approaches the mansion on its right side and then curves to the left to pass in front and wind around the left side.  It was as I began the turn approaching the left side of the house that something unusual caught my attention.  Near the side of the house, about fifty feet from where I was, was a shape that I immediately identified as a woman in a white wedding gown.  She had her back to me and appeared to be kneeling toward the house--the gown was very bunched around the ground.  I saw this figure so clearly that I was not initially frightened--perhaps due to a natual and unconscious tendency toward rationalization.  I immediately thought that there must be a wedding reception or some other type of celebration that would warrant the presence of the woman in the gown.  However, I couldn't shake the fact that she had been alone and kneeling next to the house.  By the time I reached the top of the drive, which ends at the back of the house, and noticed the absence of any type of celebration, I was absolutely convinced of what I had seen--a ghost.
If that were all there is to this particular story, it wouldn't be very convincing.  However, there are others who I later learned had seen the mysterious woman.  My friends and I used to walk deep into the woods at night to build a campfire and have fun--as college students will do on occasion.  Usually on the way, we would sing and laugh to hide the fact that the hike always seemed a little scary.  On one particular trip to our favorite area, one of the young ladies became very quiet.  When we arrived and were in the process of setting up, I asked her what was wrong.  She asked, "Didn't you see that woman sitting just off the path watching us walk past?"  I told her that I had not seen her.  I thought of my own experience not even a year earlier and asked her to describe the woman.  I could have predicted the answer--the woman wore a white gown that looked like a wedding dress.
The last time I heard of this mysterious woman was two years later, when I was a junior in college.  My study partner in Philosophy was another young lady that I had known since grade school.  One night we studied at the library until closing time, which was 10:00PM.  I decided to go home but she said she wanted to study further in the dorm lobby.  A few hours later, while she was on her way home in the car, she noticed something as she drove past what had originally been the large front property of the mansion.  It seemed like a white flash.  She dismissed it as fatigue but, as she neared the bridge that crosses Beargrass Creek, a woman in white was standing in the middle of the road.  My friend swerved to avoid hitting the woman, whom she had seen very clearly, but the figure vanished.  I told her of my experience only after she related her terrifying experience to me the next day.  I'm not sure what's there but it's the closest thing I've ever had to a real experience concerning the supernatural.

A TRUE Ghost Story

By: kansaidan@home.com

Every word of this story is TRUE. I am a believer.
In 1967, we moved into an old, early 1900s rental house. I was 15, my brother was 13, and there was my mother and our stepfather.
Just inside the front door was an expansive living area with a shiny, golden-brown wood floor. A fireplace adorned the west wall. But on the floor to the right of the fireplace was the most hideous spot, a two-foot oblong shape that was black at the center and fading to reddish brown at the edges.
I told my mother that I would remove it. "It can't be removed," she said. "People have tried." I cajoled my mother to tell me more, and, with an unusual amount of hesitancy, she finally relented.
Three years before, the landlord had told her, a man, a woman, and their children, a boy and a girl, lived in the house. One night the children heard their parents shouting, so, curious, they tiptoed down the stairs and peered out into the large room from behind a flimsy, plastic accordion door.
Their father went outside and they heard the car trunk slam. He blasted back through the front door wielding a shotgun. He knocked his wife to the floor and shot her. Without hesitation, he put the barrel in his mouth and pulled the trigger, blowing pieces of himself back into a small bedroom located behind the fireplace.
Mom said the rent was a good deal, so despite the horrific history, she paid the deposit. She hoped I wouldn't be upset about her choice of living accommodations. "Freaky story!" I commented, with teenage bravado, not really half believing it. But later, I would regret my blasé attitude.
Oppressive adequately describes the atmosphere in that house. Once, when home alone, I tried to scrub the spot with liquid cleaner, Brillo pads, and then sandpaper. All to no avail. It felt as if some unknown entity watched while I labored.
On the sunniest of days, the dark interior closed in. It seemed the windows would not allow light to enter. The house was dark, but the bedroom behind the fireplace seemed the epitome of darkness. On the hottest of summer days, I would enter the room and feel an immediate chill. We, unspoken, left it empty.
As the weeks passed, I noticed my parent’s and brothers personalities change. Once eternally happy, now my folks fought and quarreled, sometimes into the early morning hours. I recall wondering how could anyone have that much energy to argue so vehemently, for so long. Did they ever sleep? My brother became morose and alienated himself from the family. He began to leave the house at night, exiting through an upstairs window and clambering out onto the roof.
Other strange phenomena occurred. I stumbled on my brother and his two friends in the old dirt basement under the kitchen playing with a ouija board. "Where did you find it?" I asked. They told me it was in the dirt behind the furnace. I watched as they asked questions while the planchet zipped around the board.
A strange malevolence gripped us. My mother fell ill with grand mal epileptic seizures. A hideous boil erupted on the calf muscle of my left leg. I heard mysterious noises, and during the evening hours, flitting shadows skirted at the periphery of my vision.
One afternoon, mom cried out, "Come here. Look at this!" When I arrived at her bedroom closet door, she swung it open. On the floor sat a lifelike doll. I am not exaggerating when I say the doll was surrealistically human appearing: it was about 3-feet tall, porcelain skin with all the color and nuances of flesh, bushy, thick hair done up in brown ringlets, and those eyes--glistening, moist, large blue eyes that I swore followed me around the room. Shirley Temple came to mind. "Where did you get THAT?" I asked. It just showed up, she said, out of the blue.
As incidents accelerated, and temperaments deteriorated, mom finally said, "We're moving!" They packed up everything and moved the furniture up to a new place.
For some reason, because of a test, perhaps a school project, or the new house was just too far away, I couldn't leave the house on that last night and move up to the new place. I told mom that it would be more convenient if I spent the night alone in the old rental. They could pick me up, along with the last few boxes of goods, after school the next day.
So, after hanging out with a friend, I arrived at the house around 9pm. (To this day, I don't know why I decided to stay overnight alone. I must have had a good reason!) I made a bed on the floor in front of the fireplace. Not a living soul was in the house. A few boxes were piled near the wall opposite the fireplace and me. I extinguished all the lights and settled in to sleep. If I had moved my feet 12 inches to the north, they would have touched that onerous spot. Somehow, soon, I was fast asleep.
My eyes fluttered, and I groggily awoke. Something wasn't right. When I had dozed off earlier, the house was black and dark as an unlit coal mine, but now I detected light. I glanced at my alarm clock. It said 3:15am.
A ghostly muted yellow, pulsating light cascaded across the floor, over the boxes, and up the wall. A gray shadow filled its center. I froze with fear pondering the question of who turned on the light, but soon succumbed to curiosity.
I rose up on my elbow and gazed past the spot, past the doorway of the cold bedroom, and toward my mother's bedroom where the light emanated from. In the doorway, silhouetted by light rays from within her room, stood the doll, its arms outstretched, and even though it was backlit, I could see a pair of glowing eyes.
Needless to say, I didn't sleep. I didn't move. At sunrise, I jumped up and dashed out of the house never to return.
