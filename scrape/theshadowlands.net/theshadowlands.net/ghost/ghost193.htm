Changing Sounds in Oklahoma

By: jsevers_ok@yahoo.com
 

This event happened in the late summer of ’62 or ’63.
The evening was just like any other school night.  My
brother and father were in the living room watching
television, my mom and sister were in mom’s bedroom
sewing or something.  I had homework to do, so I was
sitting at the dinning room table.  I recall that
evening clearly enough that I remember I was doing a
math assignment.  My uncle was staying with us and had
been for a couple of months.  My uncle always enjoyed
sitting on the front porch or out on the lawn away
from the house.  The evening was already dark and with
summer still in the air, the front door and a few
windows were open.  I wasn’t paying much attention to
anything except the math assignment.  Perhaps my uncle
tried to get the attention of my dad or brother first
but for whatever reason he came to me and asked me to
come outside. Speaking in Creek, my uncle said he
wanted me to listen to something strange that was
happening. Everyone in my family spoke the Creek
(Muskogee) Indian language all the time when we were
home. I followed him out the front screen door, out to
the far front of the lawn.  We lived in the country
and at the time we lived on an eighty-acre plot of
land.  The end of our mowed lawn was a good fifty
yards from the front porch.  I stood with my arms
folded and just as I was about to ask Uncle Joe what I
was listening for, he said, “Listen”.  The front of
the house faces east so to the Northeast the sound
came of a tomcat meowing, as male cats do when going
off on a hunt.  The meowing started a ways down the
oiled road that went out to the oil pumps on the North
side of our property.  Nothing quite strange about
that for we had several cats living in or underneath
some utility buildings near the house.  Oddly, the
meowing changed to the sound of a dog howling.  The
howling was a strange sound.  I have gone hunting with
my dogs many times in the woods behind our home but
the howling I listened to that night was truly
strange.  The howling was coming closer to the front
yard.  Just then, the howling changed to the sound of
a hooting owl.  The hooting was coming closer to the
house.   Then the hooting changed to the tomcat and
then to the howling again.  When all of sudden
everything was silent.  Only the summer night insects
kept up their hissing.  My uncle and I stood for a
while longer waiting for the sounds to start again but
they never did.

I told of what my uncle and I heard that night on
several occasions during the following years.  No one
every said anything about having a similar experience,
not even any member of my family.  That is until, one
day, six or seven years ago, my sister, brother and I
went to visit a very close friend of our father.
During the visit, Dave began talking about how he
missed our father.  Our dad had died a couple years
before of a heart attack.  He mentioned how generous
my grandpa and dad had been to him.  Just North of our
home, up a wooded hill use to sit another house.  The
house and land belonged to my grandfather who had
passed on years before I was born.  My grandfather was
a pretty successful farmer and rancher.  He was an
important enough figure in the neighborhood that the
county appointed him Deputy Sheriff of Okfuskee
County.  Anyway, I never heard of the reason but the
house on the hill was given to Dave.  Dave began
telling us about the adventures and mishaps he and dad
had shared growing up in rural Oklahoma.  While Dave
was reminiscing he began to tell of something he heard
one night as he sat on his porch.  He told of hearing
the very same thing my uncle and I heard years after.
I told Dave of what Uncle Joe and I had heard for the
first time that evening.  Until then he too didn’t
know of anyone else hearing the same sounds.
I don’t know what we heard on the two separate
occasions in Oklahoma. However, I distinctly remember
what sounds my ears captured.  I believe there are
spirits on this Earth and it is we lucky few who get a
chance to see or hear them.
Thanks for listening….
