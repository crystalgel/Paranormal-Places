My haunting experience

By: MagMug6531@aol.com
 

On a summer night when I was eight years old, I awoke to find a woman
standing by my bed looking out the window onto the street.  One hand held the
curtain back and the light from the street illuminated the form from the neck
down.  The face was in the shadows and I couldn't see it ( I wear glasses for
myopia).  The figure was solid (not transparent at all) and was wearing a
long dress with long sleeves.  The fabric had a print of very small flowers
all over on a dark background.  At first I thought I was dreaming, so I
closed one eye then opened it.  The figure was still there.  I did the same
with the other eye and then both eyes, first closing then opening them.  The
figure remained.  It didn't move and I didn't speak to it.  I didn't feel
threatened or scared in any way.  I realized it could be my mother checking
up on me and she might get mad if I spoke because it would mean that I had
been awake.  So I went back to sleep.
The following morning I went downstairs to breakfast and asked my mother if
she had been in to check on me the night before.  She said no.  When my older
sister came down, I asked her the same question.  She also said no.  My
mother told me I must have been dreaming.  I told her about my "eye trick"
and she said I must've dreamt that too.  My dad just laughed and said "it
must've been a ghost!".
Thirty eight years later, I can remember that event so clearly.  I know I
wasn't dreaming.  I don't know if it was a "ghost" or maybe an angel (I'm
Catholic) but I do know it was real.  No one in my family had passed away, so
if it was a ghost, it belonged to the house, not the family.  That experience
sparked an interest in the paranormal for me and I started reading about the
subject.  I subsequently had another experience in my teens and have several
friends who also experienced "weird" happenings in that house.
My parents sold the house shortly after I got married and another family
lives there now.  I've often wondered if they have had paranormal
experiences.  I guess I'll never know.  Thanks for listening.
