Asserting your Rights

By: Merwynel@aol.com
 

    I have been reading the stories on your site for a while now and decided to share an experience of my own.  I'm afraid it is not particularly exciting or dramatic, but I feel it's a good example of the attitude you need to take when dealing with spirits.
    I have been told on more than one occasion, by people who claimed psychic abilities, that there are spirits present in my home.  This really comes as no huge shock as the core of the house is a farmhouse that is over one hundred and twenty years old  Although I have never seen a ghost myself, I am very familiar with the sort of chilled feeling that people describe when they are in the presence of spirits, it is not truly the same feeling as normal reaction to temperature, but something that seems more internal, and comes and goes independently of environmental changes, and I have very commonly experienced this sensation, usually beginning before someone else remarks about their perceptions of something "otherworldly".
     Several years ago, one summer morning, I had come home in the early morning from working the night shift.  I was getting undressed for bed, and placed my bedroom door in a three quarter closed position that I usually keep it in to provide some cross ventilation.
     Let me explain that my bedroom is a rectangular room approximately 10 foot wide by 16 foot long  There is a set of double windows on the far end of the room; my bed is crossways in front of the windows with the head on the longer wall.  The door was on the other end of the room (remodeling has relocated it since then), and due to irregularities from different additions to the house, there was an approximate four-inch step down when entering the room from the hallway. At the time of this incident there was no central air in the house so the only cooling method was the open windows.
     As I was getting ready for bed, I saw the door swing shut, rather firmly from the three quarter position.  At first I dismissed this as just being the breeze, as I was feeling a slightly chilled feeling on what was a rather warm morning, even though I didn't really notice much in the way of air current.  I was very tired and somewhat groggy and only wished to get to bed as soon as possible.  I put my door back into the position I had had it in and went back to getting ready for bed. Almost immediately the door swung shut again very firmly. Even though I still really did not notice a breeze, the door swung quite freely on its hinges, and I did not think much of the fact that it kept shutting.  I then took one of my work boots, the common style most everyone is familiar with that laces up about nine inches above the ankle, and placed it with the toe section underneath the door and the heel towards the doorway, and repositioned the door to the three quarter position I wanted it to be in. Moments later the door drug the boot across the carpeted floor, and closed as far as it could with the boot in the way.  Now at that pint I realized that there was certainly no breeze present that could exert that amount of force, and the "chill" I was experiencing was not the normal environmental kind, nor was it in any way cold enough that morning for me to be experiencing a normal chill.
     Now I am not a person who likes to have his sleep interfered with, nor do I particularly like to have my plans, of any kind, thwarted.  Besides, all of my reading and conversations regarding the supernatural and hauntings have always indicated that you have to assert your rights to control your domain when challenged by spirits.  With this in mind, I grabbed up a heavy (approximately 12 lbs.) "Thor" hammer I had cast from aluminum years before in shop class, and placed the head of it underneath the door, with the handle sticking up between the door and the doorway.  Stepping back I then witnessed the door drag this heavy hammer (12 LBS!!!) across the floor the same way it had my boot, until again the door was as far shut as it could be without actually removing the hammer from underneath it.  At that point, becoming somewhat angry, I took the hammer out from underneath the door, placed the door into a two-thirds closed position (slightly more open than I really wanted) and waited.  Within seconds the door started to shut again.  At this point I pointed at the door and said loudly and firmly "NO!”  The door stopped moving and stayed perfectly still.  I stood there for a few moments longer watching the door and it did not move again.  I then said "thank you" and went on to bed.  I think it is important for people to understand that in most cases of encountering a spirit in your home, you simply have to assert your right to be master of your home.  I can't promise that it will always be the complete answer in all cases, but I believe it to be the best way to begin with dealing with a disagreement with a spirit in your home.

Since the posting of my story (#222) on your website, I have had several inquiries via email as to the history of the house and other such questions.  Rather than try to answer each email individually, I figure it would be simpler to post a follow-up submission to address these questions and to appease the curiosity of your readers:
First of all, I titled this story #222 as this was the address of the house.  Through some of my research into numerology, I have discovered that the number 2 is synonymous with spirits.  This house seemed to have a triple-whammy, so to speak.  Go figure.
I began doing research on the property while we were still living there.  A great deal of the documentation I had gathered during my research has, unfortunately, disappeared.  As I touched upon in my original posting, my computer would often have files mysteriously go missing and the hard drive had crashed several times.  In addition, many of the backup discs I had created of my files were also subsequently blank.  Whatever forces hover over that house apparently had a problem with my writing about them (I began keeping a journal in 1996).  They also interfered with my research quite frequently.  Also, many documents (including the journal) and backup files were destroyed when the plumbing exploded and the house was flooded.  However, I have a fairly good memory and will fill you in on what I have found as best I can recall.
The house was originally built in 1971 and had only one owner up until 1984.  The only death I could attribute to the house through public records and newspaper accounts, was the death of the original owner who died in 1984 at the age of 84.  Odd, isn't it -- 84 and 84?  Upon his death the house passed to one of the original owner's children (his daughter and her husband).  In 1984, they applied for several permits to remodel the house and, at that time, added on the screened Florida Room and dug a 16' x 32' swimming pool.  The daughter and her husband lived in the house for just shy of 2 years then put it up for sale.  According to public records, from 1986 until 1994 (when we purchased the house), the house had been bought and sold a total of 6 times.  Since 1998, when we gave the house back to the bank and it was sold at auction, there have been exactly 4 owners.  At present time, the house is vacant.
The neighborhood that the house was built in was one of relatively small size.  It consisted of one street (a dead-end, ironically enough) that had approximately 22 houses built on it (here we go with the number 2, again).  The odd thing about these 22 houses, is that there was a rash of "for sale" signs dotting the yards of the houses up and down this street during the number of years that we lived in the neighborhood.  But, then again, my [former] next door neighbor (a retired Army Colonel) has lived in his house since 1971 and still lives there today.  Who's to say?  I've often wondered if this plat of land was, at one time, a cemetery.  I say this because, at the head of the neighborhood, stood an old Episcopalian Church.  Often times cemeteries were added on behind churches.  This is only a theory, mind you, as I have not been able to find any documentation through public records of a cemetery ever having been there.  All I do know, is that the problems with this house seemed to have begun around 1984 when the swimming pool was dug.
I made several attempts to contact some of the previous 6 owners of the house, but could find none that were willing to talk to me about the property.  They either did not return my phone calls (or emails) or refused to talk to me outright.  The people we bought the house from claimed to have no knowledge whatsoever of any "hauntings".  I believe that to be a case of serious denial (they seemed a little nervous at my having brought up the subject).
Now, to address the questions about whether or not we ever invited a "psychic evaluation" or "paranormal investigation" into the property.  The answer is NO.  We never felt the need.  To be quite frank, 3 of the 6 people that lived in the house at the time (ie: my family members), possess psychic abilities.  Myself included.  You may remember from my original posting that I mentioned that I had experienced spirits before, which is why I would routinely confront them when they would play their pranks.  I have had many experiences with spirits in my lifetime, beginning in my childhood.  It seems that where ever I go, I run into some.  As a matter of fact, the house I currently live in has 2 spirits residing in it.  I discovered, through research (about a year or so after having purchased it), that a murder occurred here.  That, of course, is another story which I won't get into.  Do I have rotten luck with houses, or what?!
