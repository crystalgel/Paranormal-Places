My Many Strange Experiences

By: cdasilva1@sprint.ca

I started experiencing strange things at a very young age.  My earliest recollection was when I was around 5 or 6 yrs of age.  I grew up in Scarborough, Ontario.  While sleeping one night ( I shared a bed with my oldest sister) I awoke to see a very tall man, dressed all in black standing beside our bed looking down at us.  He wore a jacket one would see in a photo from the 17-1800's and had, I think a sort of top hat.  I remember being terrified. I tried waking my sister but did not want to move much to really attract his attention.  The next morning I asked my parents who it was that came to our room in the middle of the night all dressed in black.  Nobody believed me. I remember being terrified while living in this house and was thrilled to learn we were moving.
Nothing significant happened after that until I was married and became pregnant with my daughter.  My next strange encounter happened while staying at my in-laws home as they had gone away and my sister-in-law was to afraid to stay in the house on her own.  While my husband and sister-in-law were at work (it was around 9:00 am) I was lying in bed about to get up when a heard a voice in the diningroom say "Where's Brandy" ( the family dog who was tied up outside but hadn't barked to indicate someone had come to the house)
My first thought was that someone had broken into the house, so I pretended to be asleep hoping that if they saw me they would run out. All of a sudden my body became very tense and I could sense that someone was standing at the end of the bed watching me. This feeling lasted for approximately 2 minutes when all of a sudden my body relaxed and I knew that I was alone again.
I opened my eyes and sure enough no one was there, all the doors  were locked and I realized that the dog had not made a sound.  Talking with my in-laws, they stated that they had sometimes seen a person in another part of the house out of the corner of their eyes.  I also remembered sitting in the livingroom, which was at the front of the house, and could swear someone was walking up the outside  stairs  leading  to the front door only to look and find no one there.
My husband and I bought our first home, a townhouse, when my daughter turned one.  Whenever I laid down in the afternoon, with my daughter for her afternoon nap, I would always here someone pacing back and forth in the unfinished part of the basement.  Whenever I would get up, and go part way down the main floor stairs, the pacing would stop.  One day my husband and I planned to be away for the night, but at the last minute decided to go home instead.  We arrived home just after midnight. While waiting for my husband to get out his keys, I noticed the front curtains moving as though someone had been looking out and had just released them. I mentioned this to my husband and we both thought maybe someone had broken in. He already had the keys in the lock and was determined to check.  As he tried to open the door he said it felt like someone was pushing the door from the opposite side. He gave a heavy push against the door and suddenly there was this bright flash of light from inside the house.  Looking inside we neither saw nor heard anyone running away.
Looking around I noticed that our staircase light was on and mentioned it to my husband.  He responded that I must have left it before we left. I answered " That light has been broken for months, why would it come on after all this time,"  My husband tries to justify the flash of light as coming from the parking lot behind our townhouse but if you could see the set up of our townhouse  you would understand that this theory was impossible.  For the light to reach our front door it would have to go though a 6 foot privacy fence, down a small hill and around three corners before it would even reach the  doorway.
I had to go for some minor surgery, the next morning my husband picked me up at the hospital, dropped my off at my sisters penthouse apartment and left.  I fell asleep on the sofa.  I woke up around 11:00 am and lay there with my eyes closed thinking I should get up and have something to eat when I felt something hit the back of the sofa (it was set up so that you walked behind it to go down the hall to the bedrooms) as if someone had banged it with their hip.  My body went very tense. I could then hear someone walk from behind the sofa, around the coffee table to stand in front of my face.  I was lying with my back to the back of the sofa and my head was on a pillow.  Suddenly, someone hit the pillow just in front of my face twice.  My body then totally relaxed and I knew that I was now alone.  Checked out the apartment and nothing.
Next, while staying with the same sister and her family one Christmas Eve, my daughter and I slept in my nephews room while my husband slept on the couch in the livingroom.  My husband is not the type to seek his own comfort at my  expense yet this night he came into our room saying he could not sleep and would I exchange places with him.  Not thinking this strange or even question him, we traded places.  I fell asleep but woke up a short time later. Looking at the clock it read 12:14 AM. I closed my eyes thinking I would have a hard time falling asleep again when I felt something crawling over the arm of the sofa where my head was.  Thinking it was one of the children sneaking in to see if Santa had come, I was about to open my eyes and yell "what are you doing out of bed", when I felt a shock touch me on the top of my head and travel to about my waist. Nervous, I again tried to open my eyes only to get another shock.  Truly scared now, I thought I will try this once more and if it happens again, I am going to scream bloody murder. I opened my eyes and nothing, looked at the clock and it read 12:16 am.  I ran into my husband and said. "don't ask me why, just change places with me."  He got up, said nothing, and promptly fell asleep in the livingroom.
I can't explain why any of these things happened to me but I know that I was not asleep when these things happened.  My youngest nephew was terrified of that apartment and he was around 2 - 4 when they lived there and there were parts of the apartment he would not sit, be rocked or go alone.
