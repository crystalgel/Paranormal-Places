Hospital Ghost Story

By: wbrison@rotech.com

I am a Respiratory Therapist. While working graveyard shift in a hospital on the Texas side of the Red River. I experienced what I feel was a brush with the supernatural. R.T's help patients to breathe better. But also to provide emergency care to bring them back from their visit to Eternity and beyond.The Sixth floor of the hospital is where nursing home patients and patients who are chronically ill or had un treatable cancer where usually
admitted. If you talk to anybody who worked in the hospital there would always be a room or a bed that seem to have an unlucky results for those patients who were placed there. There was such a room on the sixth floor.
After performing a code earlier on a patient whom passed on the sixth floor. I was called to look at a patient in THE ROOM next to the nurses station. The Patient was very elderly and seem very confused upon first initial contact. While assessing the patient the patient was talking in tongues and pledging her allegiance to the dark underworld of Satan. This patient's arms were tied to her bed to prevent her from hurting her self. The patient was frothing at the mouth and her eyes would roll behind her head exposing only the white of her eyes. Needless to say
it was very unsettling. We left the patients room to look at the patients chart when then the patient's call light went off in the patients room numerous times that night. what was weird about it is that the patient was unable to press it because her hands were tied up. Later that night we coded the patient and she passed away and was moved to the funeral home that morning. The next night I showed up for my shift again and was up on the sixth floor talking to the nurses that took care of the patient the night before. When the call light went off in the room that the patient was in the night before. We turned on the light walked in to the empty patient room and reset the pt call. The room felt unusually cold and still. We returned to the nurses station when the call light went off again in the same room , after resetting it again. We returned to the nurses station when the EMERGENCY call light went off again. This
time the call box had been pulled out of the wall and was lying on the ground. This was very unsettling because no person was in the room and because of the close proximity of the room to the nurses station that no-one could have done this without being seen. Maintenance was called and found nothing unusual in the room. No patients were admitted to that room that night.  We assumed that the patient wanted us to know that she was still around was not done scaring us. We looked at the time and it was the same time we coded her the night before. After this happened My senses were enhanced and the graveyard shift had a whole new meaning.

Testing the Waters

By: gangrel@bbc.net

 

    One night, a few years ago in Kentucky, I was watching tv in my
bedroom. It was pretty dark except for the tv as the room was located in
the basement, but I was wide awake. It started getting chilly. Chilly
enough to get bundled up. As I was wrapping myself up, I looked over
toward the far wall, and a lady was walking toward me. (I get the heebie
jeebies now just thinking about it). It scared me so much I made that
noise you only hear in Hollywood movies, when you try to scream but
inhale at the same time. She took three steps toward me (that's
important, I'll tell you why in just a minute) and vanished. Well,
vanished is a strong word. She faded out very quickly. She looked
thirtyish, shoulder length dark hair, and was wearing a blue dress that
went to her ankles. A modern dress, as well as a modern hairstyle.

    A soon as she faded out, an overwhelming sense that she was sorry
totally engulfed me. And I thought to myself that I felt sad that I
scared her off by my reaction. I've been (this is somewhat embarrassing
to say. I've always believed in sixth sense, paranormal, things like
that. But I was a deputy at one time, and want to be a physicist after I
get my Ph.D. These don't go well with paranormal.) sensitive to things
since I was a child. I've gone to houses that made me nauseous just to
be there, and sometimes certain rooms in some houses give me the
willies. But this was different. The feelings I got were all wrong.

    I did a little research and found that ghosts can send an olive
branch to a person. A ghost to test the waters for them, if you will.
And I felt in that room that I was needed. I brought in a friend to
help, and we tried EVP. We got nothing. We tried pictures and video, and
still nothing. I was an idiot and tried Ouija (people, Parker Brothers
may make it, but it's as dangerous as a nuclear bomb. I recommend
against Ouija) and didn't have the thing move once. But something flat
out told me that if I used it anymore, terror would only be the
beginning (I also felt that who I had contaced through the thing wasn't
her). So I had no answers but what I felt. After a few weeks, 2 or 3 it
seems to me, the feeling that she was still around faded.

    I felt this: she was supposed to test the waters, and I failed the
test. There is a message out there I won't get. I felt that it was a
message from a relative.

    She came through the wall of my bedroom. Her three steps would have
put her right on top of me, but they didn't. Her third step put her in
the middle of my room. The wall dissolved around her. Was it an after
image from the TV on my retinas? No. I know what they are like. So do
you. This was crystal clear, and her size would overlap the size if the
tv screen from my position in the room. Between two eyes and the TV, I
had fairly good parallax.

    The room felt clean when when I moved in, and a few short weeks
after this incident it felt clean. I never found out who she was, but
I'll never forget her. Perhaps I'll get the chance to ask her face to
face someday.

A Tale of Two Entities

By: katerrell@buckman.com
 
 

I wrote to you about my grandfather's ghost, but thought you might be one of
the few people who could appreciate the other "entities" that have been part
of my life.
When I was a child, my mother's grandparents gave me an old brass framed
bed. They'd rescued it from a burned down house in the country near where
they lived, cleaned it up, painted it, and for years used it in their guest
room.
I often got the strangest feeling that I wasn't alone in my bedroom, but of
course no one else was there. I had the strongest belief that "whatever" was
causing that sensation had something to do with that bed. It was never
threatening; in fact, it was almost rather comforting. I tried to tell my
mother about it, but she ascribed it to a bright child's overactive
imagination.
My dog, however, sensed it as well. There would often be the sound of the
front door opening and closing (while in actuality, it had remained locked
and closed), and she would wag her tail, obviously watching SOMETHING go up
the stairs to my bedroom. Even my mother could not deny that there was
something going on.
It was rather natural that I'd begin talking to this invisible "person". It
was important to me to convince my mother that what I had described as a
presence was real, and I told him I needed his help to do that. He never
spoke to me, not even silently, but I got the feeling he was amused. Hoping
for something tangible to show my mother, I called her into my room. I had
some scarves and bandanas tied around the foot of the bed. With my mother
watching, several of the scarves lifted, by themselves, and then dropped
back into position.
The hair on my mother's arm stood straight up, her eyes grew round, and she
left the room, complaining about my "convincing little trick". She
maintained that it had to have been a ruse of some kind.... but after that,
she stopped treating me as if I were making up a story about the presence
that came with my bed.
When I was a teenager, I became interested in occult and spiritual topics. I
played around with what I thought was "magic" and inadvertently summoned
something that was NOT pleasant. It brought with it the sensation of being
watched, but it wasn't a comfortable feeling. This presence was mildly
hostile, and the corner of my room where I would "sense" it would be cold,
even though there was no breeze or vent near. A few times, looking back at
that corner in my vanity mirror, I could have sworn I saw two glowing blue
spot where eyes should have been.
It was a hostile presence, but it didn't actually do anything other than
scare my dog, give me and my  mother the creeps, and periodically frighten
my friends away. (They'd never explain why they didn't want to come up to my
room, other than that they sometimes felt "something creepy" up there.) It
wasn't always there all the time, just as the other spirit-creature wasn't
present all the time, but we all definitely knew when it WAS there.
It didn't do anything, until I decided to DO something about it. I decided
to try to banish that unpleasant presence, or exorcise it. Apparently, all I
did was enrage the thing, because for the first time it affected material
objects. My doll collection flew off the bookshelves toward me, as did books
and some trinkets. The cold presence also physically moved towards me, and
for the first time I was truly frightened.
Then the cold entity stopped, as if it were blocked, and there seemed to be
a struggle of some kind.... the comforting presence that I'd grown up with,
the one associated with my bed... well, I could swear it was protecting me.
The cold thing left, and I made up an excuse as to why all the things in my
room were lying in the floor in the center of the room.
Both things followed my mother and me through several moves, from one
apartment to another, so we knew that it had to do with either one of us, or
with something that we moved with us. When I moved out to live with my
fiancé, neither of them moved with me. (Of course, I'd left the bed.)
The cold thing's presence dwindled, but when my mother took that old brass
bed as her own again, the other presence became strong again.
One night, when my mother was particularly upset and crying about something,
she felt a body's weight on the mattress, and masculine arms went around her
waist. She was petrified, convinced that someone had broken into the house
and was preparing to molest her. Finally, she summoned the courage to move,
... and no one was there.
The next day, she spoke aloud, telling "him" (we called him "George" by
then) to go away, that he had frightened her horribly.
Well, apparently that was enough for George, because his comforting presence
has never been felt since. My mother is sad that she spoke so hastily. She
misses him, and says that she knows he was only trying to comfort her, and
that she over-reacted.
On the other hand, the other presence still every now and then re-occurs. My
own son won't sleep in that room (that used to be mine), and will rarely
even go into it alone, even in the daytime.
