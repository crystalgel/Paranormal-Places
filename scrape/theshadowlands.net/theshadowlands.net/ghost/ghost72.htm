One Dog in a Million

By: "Bobbie" <jb@macnet.com>

I have had a wonderful experience with the

spirit of my beloved dog. Danny is a Cocker Spaniel. I had wanted a Cocker all

of my life. I finally got the opportunity when I received a settlement from an

insurance co. My husband told me that he wanted me to get something that I had

always wanted. Needless to say the Cocker was my first response. That very day

we found a news paper and looked for puppies. We found one listing close by and

went there to look. HE was sitting there in the corner looking at me with the

most beautiful eyes I had ever seen.After that, Danny was my constant companion. We

went everywhere together and except for work, he was always by my side. We took

him on all our camping trips and sightseeing excursions. He was a great traveler

and loved being on the road. The minute I thought about leaving for a trip,

Danny would prance around me just waiting to hear the jingle of his dog collar.

It got to the point that I had to put his collar on just to calm him down so I

could get everything ready. Danny was more then a friend, he understood my moods

and always knew the right thing to do to cheer me up or settle me down. He

rejoiced in my happiness When I became disabled Danny made sure that when we

went out, I could keep up with him. The years went much to fast. Before I knew

it he was 13 years old and slowing down. Now it was my turn to slow down for

him. When the end came it was so sudden I felt out of step with reality for a

while. He had been ill and suddenly one evening he started having breathing

difficulties. I put him in the truck with me and I took off for the Vet's

office. He was unconscious by the time I got there and died about 15 minutes

later. I was devastated. I was so overcome

with grief that all I wanted to do was die with him. I still can't talk about

him with great ease but it is much better now. One night about a week after he

passed over, I had a dream about him. He came running into my arms

and I felt his overwhelming love for me. I held him and cried into the soft fur

on his back. He looked just as he did as a young dog. He had been blind in one

eye, but now his eye was fine and he was so happy! He stayed with me until I

stopped crying and then turned and ran off. He was really there with me. Our

spirits met and he was able to comfort me enough to be able to cope with his

loss. I think my part in this was actually an out of body experience, because it

was to real for a regular dream or a lucid dream, which I have had on several

occasions. Another outcome of this meeting was the overwhelming desire to get

another Cocker. I wasn't going to get another because I was afraid I would

always be comparing him with Danny. But I suddenly HAD to find another one. Even

that search was a little unusual. We live in Oregon and have access to the two

largest cities, Portland and Salem. I looked in 6 news papers and found only one

listing for Cocker pups! There are usually dozens of them. I called the lady

that had them and was at her door step and hour later. DJ came home with

me. He is a great little guy and I love him dearly. He has secured his own spot

in my heart. It's great to have him home

again. I realize that this isn't your typical ghost

story, but it does show that our pet companions are with us always. If you don't

include this story I don't mind. It's be cathartic just writing

it!

The House On Central Ave

By: morninglily@webtv.net

This story has been told to me by Mother and Grandmother over the years.

Today they still have nightmares about what occured at this house they

lived in. Here is their story as it was told to me....

When my mother was 7 years old in (1957), my grandparents rented a house

from a young man who said he was renting it out on behalf of his Father

who recently moved to Alabama. He had only request: He wanted to keep

some belongings in the basement and he locked the door with a padlock.

He told them never to try to get in the basement. My Grandfather

thought this was reasonable enough and he agreed.

Soon after, strange things began to happen in the house. But, they only

happened when my Grandfather was not present. He worked graveyard at

the time so My Grandma and Mother were left alone at night. My Mother

was the first to see the ghost. She awoke one night to see a shadow of

something standing next to her bed. As she looked up she seen that it

was headless. There was nothing above its shoulders! She screamed and

went to her Mothers room. Grandmother could not calm her that night,

but she did believe her because she had felt it too.

Mom slept in her Mothers room after that. One night, it jerked their

covers off. Grandmother immediately got up an looked around the room

for a reasonable expanation, when suddenly she turned and seen it for

herself. She nearly fainted. After that, she and my Mother went to bed

everynight with the door locked and moved the dresser in front of the

door thinking they could prevent its entrance. Needless to say that did

not work. Over time, the ghost grew stronger as did it's attacks.

Window started opening in the dead of winter, they heard footsteps

walking from the bedroom to the basement door and going down. The chair

in the living room would rock all of the time. Doors would slam, and at

night it would take their covers. At times you could hear it breathing

heavily. My grandmother could not take it anymore, she tried so hard

to convince my grandfather, but nothing worked. He simply did not

believe in such things.

How it all came to an end: There was a large rust colored stain in the

bedroom, that my grandmother could never remove. There were also stains

in the hall leading to the basement door. After a period of time, she

became convinced it was blood. This is how she came to that

asumption....

The young man returned on day from Alabama to collect his things. She

asked him about the stains that she could not remove. He told her that

while his wife was in, she miscarried in the basement and managed to

drag herself to the bedroom where she collapsed. She lived, but they

were never able to get all of the blood up. Well, my Grandmother

halfway bought his story. Later that night, they invited him to stay,

before heading back to Alabama. He did stay, but refused to sleep in

the house. Mind you, it was winter time. The next day after he left,

Grandma asked the neighbors a few questions. Why did the boys Father

never come around and why did he give up his home? The neighbors

reported that the Father and son fought constantly. The old man was a

brutal drunk. One night they thought they heard a gunshot, but wasn't

sure. Shortly after that, they noticed the old man wasn't around. When

they questioned the boy, he said that his dad left for Alabama and that

he was moving there too. A few days later, they noticed the boy had his

basement dug out. He said he was cementing it . Well, that was enough

for my grandmother. After hearing all of this, she went to the house

and decided to bust the lock off of the door. When she got down in the

basement she noticed in the corner, there was a real bad sinkhole in the

cement. It was the perfect shape of a grave. Next, all of the lights

went out on her and she heard footsteps. She freaked out. That night

she told Grandpa the whole story. He still didn't buy it, but that

night, he came face to face with it. He almost had a heart attack. He

immediately made plans for them to move.

The night before they left, all of the windows in the house would not

stay shut. Doors kept opening and slamming. Grandpa got up no less

than four times to shut the windows. Finally, after an unseen hand

grabbed my Mothers foot and tried to drag her in the floor did Grandpa

have enough. They slept the rest of the night in their car even though

they had 3 inches of snow on the ground. To this day, my Mother will

not go near that house. Later a preacher and his family moved in it.

Their daughter later was admitted to a mental hosptial. It was because

she was being attacked by an unseen force in the house and her parents

thought she was just crazy, that was at least until my grandmother told

them her story of the house.....
